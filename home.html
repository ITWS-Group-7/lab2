TRIVIA

Ever wonder who writes the laws we live by? The legislative branch! Congress, which has Senate and House of Representatives are part of this.

Wait so the President doesn't make laws? Nope! Article II says the President is part of the executive branch. The President's job is just to make sure the laws are carried out.

Article III is all about the Supreme Court. The Supreme Court is the most powerful court, and they promise fairness and equality.

So, you might be thinking - "What about my state?" Well, that's where Article IV comes in - the federal government takes care of states and states take care of each other - the UNITED states!

If you want to add more amendments to the constitution, you can! (if you get two-thirds of the vote of course)

Article VI basically says that the constitution is THE law of the land! It makes sure that we support it and help our government when they are in need, for example if there is a national debt.

How do states adopt the constitution? In Article VII, if 9 states approve, then the constitution applies for those states!

You've probably heard someone say, "Free Speech!" Well, they are talking about the first Amendment, which allows free speech, press, and right to protest.

The second amendment allows you to have weapon to protect yourself. But, there's a lot of debate on what exactly this means in our world today.

In a nutshell, the third amendment says that a soldier cannot live in your home during peace time unless you approve of it!

Well, we know that law enforcement can search for clues, but they don't have complete free-reign. The fourth amendment is there to ensure that all searches are done properly!

Have you ever heard of the phrase "Plead the Fifth"? Well if you haven't its what people say when they don't want to answer questions that could accuse them of a crime.

This ensures that people who are accused of a crime have the right to a fair trial. It also makes sure that the people on the jury are fair and unbiased.

The seventh amendment basically allows an indvidual and corporations to have a fair trial in federal court.

This makes sure that if a person is charged with a crime, that their punishements are not too mean!

So are the only rights we have are those that are in the constitution? Nope! Amendment 9 says we have more rights than those listed! Cool!

In a nutshell, whatever rights are not only for the federal government are for the states and us!
